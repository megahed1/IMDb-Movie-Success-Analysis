<h1 align="center">🎬 IMDb Movie Success Analysis 📊</h1>

<p align="center">
A Data Analysis Project to uncover the secrets behind movie success using 10,000+ films dataset
</p>

<hr>

<h2>📌 Business Scenario</h2>

<p>
A major <b>Film Production Studio</b> aims to redefine its production strategy for the next 5 years.
As a <b>Lead Data Analyst</b>, I analyzed a dataset of <b>10,000+ movies</b> to extract actionable insights
on financial success and audience reception.
</p>

<p><b>Key Questions:</b></p>
<ul>
  <li>Does a higher budget guarantee better ratings?</li>
  <li>Who are the most profitable directors?</li>
  <li>How have movie trends evolved over decades?</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>🛠️ Data Engineering & Cleaning</h2>

<ul>
  <li><b>Irrelevant Data Removal:</b> Removed unnecessary columns (homepage, tagline, keywords, IDs).</li>
  <li><b>Financial Normalization:</b> Used <code>budget_adj</code> and <code>revenue_adj</code> for fair comparison across decades.</li>
  <li><b>Data Integrity:</b> Handled missing values and removed duplicates.</li>
</ul>

<hr>

<h2>💡 Key Insights</h2>

<h3>📉 The Budget Myth</h3>
<p>
Correlation between budget and rating = <b>0.09</b><br>
➡️ High budget ≠ High rating
</p>

<h3>🎯 Top Performing Directors</h3>
<p>
Top directors based on <b>Adjusted Revenue (post-1997)</b> include:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>James Cameron</li>
  <li>Peter Jackson</li>
</ul>

<h3>💰 Revenue Outliers</h3>
<p>
Identified blockbuster movies with revenue exceeding <b>$3.5B (adjusted)</b>
to analyze success patterns.
</p>

<h3>📊 Rating Trends</h3>
<p>
Analyzed how audience expectations changed over time to define realistic KPIs.
</p>
